Risk-tier use chart for nonprofit AI

Print, post, refer back. Adapt to your organization.

Not all AI use carries the same risk. A chart that treats brainstorming the same as eligibility screening will fail in practice. This one-pager sorts common nonprofit AI uses into four tiers, with the human review requirements that go with each. Adapt the specifics to your organization's mission, data, and population.

Tier 1
Low risk
Required: human review before publication; no PII in prompts; internal transparency about AI assistance.
  • Drafting internal communications and newsletters
  • Brainstorming campaign or event ideas
  • Summarizing long documents and meeting notes
  • Grammar checking and proofreading
  • Social media drafts and variations
  • Research and literature summaries for grant prep
  • First-pass blog drafts on public topics
  • Meeting agendas and recaps from notes you've already cleaned
  • Headline and subject-line brainstorming
Tier 2
Medium risk
Required: supervisor or subject-matter expert review; documented rationale; disclosure to relevant parties where appropriate.
  • Grant application drafting and revision
  • Donor communications and personalization
  • Financial forecasting assistance
  • Job description writing
  • Translation and multilingual communications
  • Program outcome analysis using aggregate data
  • Board materials and meeting briefings
  • Policy or process explainer emails
  • Curriculum scaffolds and training outlines
Tier 3
High risk
Required: mandatory human decision-making; AI may provide information but cannot determine outcomes; explicit policy review.
  • Drafting communications to clients in distress
  • Summarizing case notes or service interactions
  • Anything touching protected health information (with BAA in place)
  • Personnel-adjacent drafting (performance, discipline-adjacent)
  • Predictive scoring of donors or prospects
  • Vendor evaluation involving sensitive data flows
  • Public statements during crises or controversies
  • AI-assisted recommendations in service planning
Tier 4
Prohibited
No AI use, regardless of tier or vendor. Errors here cause direct harm to the people you exist to serve.
  • Eligibility decisions for services, housing, or benefits
  • Crisis communications in trauma or crisis situations
  • Individual client case notes that become part of the record without explicit human authorship
  • HR disciplinary actions and termination decisions
  • Medical or psychiatric recommendations for clients
  • Resume screening that determines who gets interviewed
  • Surveillance or behavior monitoring of clients in residential settings
  • Any automated decision denying someone access to services
  • Voice cloning to impersonate staff or stakeholders

Principles that apply across every tier

1. Anonymize before you paste. No client names, no donor identities, no SSNs, no specific financial details, no PHI without a BAA in place.

2. Disclose when asked. If a funder, client, or partner asks whether AI contributed to a document, answer directly and specifically.

3. Verify factual claims. AI confidently produces things that are wrong. Any name, number, citation, or law in an AI output is unverified until you check.

4. Document who approved each new use. A short note in your shared drive saying when, why, and who signed off. The audit trail is the protection.

5. Review the chart annually. AI capabilities, vendor policies, and sector norms change. What is medium risk today may be high risk in twelve months. Update accordingly.
